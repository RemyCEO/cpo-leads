What Are Bodyguard Jobs?

Bodyguard jobs — also known as close protection (CP), executive protection (EP), or personal protection officer (PPO) roles — involve safeguarding individuals from physical threats. Bodyguards protect celebrities, corporate executives, politicians, high-net-worth families, and other at-risk individuals.

The industry has grown significantly, with demand spanning from Silicon Valley tech executives to diplomats in conflict zones. Whether you're looking for armed bodyguard work in high-risk environments or unarmed residential security, CPO Leads aggregates opportunities from across the globe.

Types of Bodyguard Jobs

Executive Protection (EP)

Protecting C-suite executives, tech founders, and business leaders. Often involves advance work, route planning, and travel security. Rates: $400-$1,200/day.

Celebrity & VIP Protection

Personal protection for entertainers, athletes, and public figures. Requires discretion, media awareness, and crowd management skills. Rates: $500-$1,500/day.

Residential Security Team (RST)

Securing private estates and family compounds. Hybrid roles combining static security with mobile protection. Often permanent or long-term contracts.

Hostile Environment (PSD)

Personal Security Detail in conflict zones — Iraq, Syria, Africa, maritime. Highest pay rates ($500-$2,000/day) but requires military/LE background.

Corporate Security

Protecting corporate events, board meetings, and executive travel. Includes threat assessment and liaison with local law enforcement.

Maritime Security

Anti-piracy and vessel protection through high-risk transit zones. Gulf of Aden, Indian Ocean, West Africa. Armed positions with premium rates.

Bodyguard Salary & Day Rates (2026)

Bodyguard pay varies dramatically based on risk level, location, and experience:

  • Entry-level (unarmed, domestic): $200-$350/day
  • Mid-level (armed, corporate EP): $400-$800/day
  • Senior (hostile environment, PSD): $600-$1,500/day
  • Specialist (maritime, advance team lead): $800-$2,000/day

Annual salaries for full-time bodyguards range from $60,000 to $200,000+, depending on the client and deployment frequency.

How to Get a Bodyguard Job

  • Get certified: Complete a recognized CPO/EP academy (minimum 4-day course). SIA licence required in the UK.
  • Build experience: Start with event security or corporate protection to build your portfolio.
  • Get firearms qualified: Armed positions pay significantly more. Obtain relevant permits (CCW, exposed firearm permit, etc.).
  • First aid certification: FREC 3 or equivalent is required for most roles.
  • Network: Join platforms like CPO Leads where verified employers post daily.
  • Specialize: EVOC driving, surveillance detection, medical skills, and language abilities all increase your value.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need military experience to be a bodyguard?
No. While military and law enforcement backgrounds are valued, many successful bodyguards come from civilian security backgrounds. A recognized CPO/EP academy certification, relevant permits, and demonstrated competence are what employers look for.
What qualifications do I need for bodyguard jobs in the UK?
You need an SIA Close Protection licence, first aid certification (FREC 3 minimum), and ideally an EVOC driving certificate. Many employers also require a valid passport and DBS check.
How much do bodyguards make?
Day rates range from $200/day for entry-level domestic work to $2,000/day for specialist hostile environment roles. Full-time bodyguards in the US and UK typically earn $60,000-$200,000+ annually depending on the client and risk level.
Are bodyguard jobs dangerous?
Risk varies enormously. Corporate EP in London is low-risk. PSD work in Iraq or maritime anti-piracy carries genuine danger. Higher-risk roles pay premium rates and require more extensive training and insurance.
